Persona Non Grata

/ˌpɜːrsənə nɒn ˈɡrɑːtə/

Definition

Persona Non Grata (Latin for "an unwelcome person") is a status applied by a host country to a foreign diplomat or other foreign national, signifying that the person is no longer acceptable and must leave the country. Under Article 9 of the 1961 Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations, a host state may at any time, and without having to explain its decision, declare any member of a diplomatic mission persona non grata. The sending state must then recall the person or terminate their functions with the mission.

This declaration is the strongest form of disapproval a host country can express towards an individual diplomat, short of severing diplomatic ties.

Origin & Context

The term is Latin and has been adopted universally in diplomatic language. Its formal incorporation into international law is primarily through the Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations (VCDR).
